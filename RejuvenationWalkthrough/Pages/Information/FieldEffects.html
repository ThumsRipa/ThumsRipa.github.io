Field Effect Overview

Field Effects are the effects that the environment has on the battle. In the main series, the closest comparison would be the terrains introduced in Generation 7, but Pokemon Reborn and Rejuvenation take this idea even further. Fields are usually determined by the location of your battle, for example the Forest Field takes place in a forest, but fields can also be created or destroyed by using certain moves. Using moves that create or destroy fields will boost the power of those moves by 1.3x. Fields created by moves such as Grassy Terrain or abilities such as Grassy Surge will not replace existing fields unless there is no field currently.

These fields have a wide array of effects on moves, abilities, and even the type chart, and are catagorized into four different types: Elemental, Telluric, Magical, or Synthetic. Generally speaking, Elemental Fields are those that are detemined by the elements, such as Grassy Terrain or Volcanic Fields. Telluric Fields are determined by the surroundings, such as Forests or Caves. Synthetic Fields are those created by the actions of humans, such as the Factory Field or the Big Top Arena. Finally, Magical Fields are created by other, otherworldly means such as the Dimensional Field or the Haunted Field.

There are four different seeds for each of the types of fields that can be used as help items to boost your Pokemon, and these seeds will be consumed after its use. For most basic fields, the seeds will give a simple stat boost and/or an effect of a move. For example, holding an Elemental Seed in the Electric Terrain will boost your Defense stat and give you effects of using the move Charge. For some fields though, this boost is magnified at a cost. An example of this is holding a Magical Seed in the Psychic Terrain will boost your Pokemon's Special Attack and Special Defense, but will also cause your Pokemon to become confused.

Many fields will add an additional typing to moves, causing it to deal two different type damages. This can lead to much more effective or much more resisted moves. For example, in the Dragon's Den, the move Smack Down gains Fire type damage. This causes it to have a 8x effectiveness against something that is Bug/Flying like a Butterfree. On the other hand, using Surf in the Electric Terrain will cause it to pick up electricity, making the move part Electric-type. Thus, ground types will be immune to the move.

Below are some terminology that may be helpful when reading about the Fields.

Field Damage: Damage caused by the field. Often, this damage will be scaled by typed, explained below:

Type Scaling: Damage dealt depends on typing. 4x Weakness = 1/2 Max HP damage. 2x Weakness = 1/4 Max HP damage. No Weakness/Resistance = 1/8 Max HP damage. 2x Resistance = 1/16 Max HP damage. 4x Resistance = 1/32 Max HP damage.

Grounded: Non-airborne Pokemon. Generally this means that the Pokemon is not a Flying-type, does not have Levitate as its ability, and is not holding an Air Balloon

SPECIAL MOVE: Lycanroc's Z-Move Splintered Stormshards removes all field effects (excluding New World and Underwater) for three turns
