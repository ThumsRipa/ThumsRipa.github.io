Immediately as you enter the right side, you'll come across the Gearen Marketplace. Here, you'll find a multitude of shops where you can buy or sell your items. The top left seller will sell you basic supplies, while the bottom left seller sells status healing items. The top right dude will sell you Pokeballs for 400, but if you buy four he will give you a Voltorb. If you are playing during the weekdays and it is daytime (11:00am-8:00pm), there will be two more vendors along the bottom row. The bottom middle seller will sell Reverse Candies and Soda Pops, while the bottom right seller will sell you Black Flutes and White Flutes.

You can also go into the Shopping Emporium. The Berry Emporium sells berries, and the Pokeball Boutique sells different Pokeballs. For now, you'll want to buy at least one Pecha Berry for later on. More variations of Pokeballs and berries will be unlocked as we progress through the story. With that done, go ahead and go back left and this time go north. You'll find patches of grass, but the encounters are the same as the left side.


This building right here is the gym. If you enter it, you'll find that the Gym Leader is currently not there, so let's explore the city a bit more. Keep going right, and you'll find a small tent with a Super Potion next to it. This is Luck's Tent. If you have been having troubles with battles or find battles too easy, you can come here to change the difficulty. This will be free the first time you enter the tent, but after that you will have to pay colored shards to change the difficulty. Once you're done, go ahead and head down and then to the right. You can battle Prima Donna Bianca.

Right past Bianca and up the stairs is Chrisola Hotel. If you sneak around the garden, you can get a TM83 - Infestation. We'll explore the hotel a bit later, so head downstairs and all the way to the right. There's a little pathway that leads up north, where there's an Iron. Head back down, and just a little ways from there Cool Guy Sim will challenge you to a battle. After battling him, head into the house with the Bellosom statues to watch a cutscene. You'll be prompted to give her a Pecha Berry, so if you haven't bought one you can buy one from the Berry Emporium. The girl will introduce herself as Veronica, AKA Venam. She'll invite you to hang out in the sewers and will take her leave. We'll follow her in a bit, but there's a few more things we can do around the city. If you do want to follow her, you'll have to battle Gang Member Rufus to the south of her house. If you want to go ahead and meet up with her at the sewers, continue down past Rufus and head right. The walkthrough will continue here. Otherwise, we'll keep exploring the city for a little while longer.

Keep going left along the road and you'll come across a four-way intersection. The last two trainers in this city are Hiker Denko who is hanging out on the bridge up top and Prima Donna Penelope who is just to the left of this intersection. There's a park to the south, but we'll explore some of the buildings first.

Head back up to Chrisola Hotel and enter it. You'll be put into a short cutscene where you're introduced to Ms. Blakeory, but you won't learn much about her before she flies away on a helicopter. You'll be able to explore the hotel now. In the lobby, you can get a Super Potion from this man on the bottom right. Go ahead and move on to the right hallway. The first open door will be occupied by a lady and her Shinx, but we can't do anything about that currently. The room all the way on the top right will contain TM21 - Frustration, which can be a very powerful Normal-type move at this point since your Pokemon aren't friendly with you yet. There's a few hidden items in the left hallway.

Once you are back in the lobby, head up to the elevator. We have access to a few floors, so head down to the basement first where the pool is. There's a few hidden items along the ground, but otherwise we'll need to have Surf to get the item in the middle. Head back to the elevator and go to the second floor. In the Casino, talk to this woman, who will complain about her addiction and throw her Coin Case away. We'll be able to retrieve it later for our own use. On the far left there is Rarity who will roll the lottery for you once a day. Head all the way down, and you'll enter the Achievement Points Shop, or the AP Shop for short. Talking to this girl will give you the Achievement Card, which you can use to check how many points you earned. The AP Shop is on the top right corner, and sells Golden Tools for you to use. These Golden Tools can be used as HMs, so make sure to buy them when you have the points to spare. That's it for this floor, head back to the elevator and head to the third floor.

The Battle Room is quite empty at the moment, but you can use this machine to rest up and heal your Pokemon. On the rooftop, there are patches of grass with different Pokemon you can catch. If you catch a Male Cherubi, we can trade it for a Buneary very soon. That's everything for this hotel, so head back out into the streets.

Outside the hotel, if you've seen Buizel run away from the Pier, you'll find it along the right edge of the city. If you interact with it, its previous trainer will come over and try to take Buizel back. Battle him, and then you can take Buizel with you if you win.

Aqua Building


Head over to Aqua Building, which is located below the Gearen Market; across the bridge and to the right. On the first floor, talking to this ranger will allow you to trade the Male Cherubi for a Buneary. The scientist by the eleveator will also give you an Ultra Ball. On the second floor, at the very bottom, you'll find an Old Rod. There's not really anywhere we can fish yet, so just hold on to it for now. I would not advise going to the third floor, but if you do choose to make sure to save beforehand.

Our final detour before we move on with the story will be Gearen Park. From Aqua Building, head down south and down the small set of stairs. Before you head into Gearen Park proper, there's a Heart Scale and a Tiny Mushroom you can pick up. Inside Gearen Park, there are a few new Pokemon here to check out, and a lot of hidden items. Of those, the Leaf Stone is probably the most useful item, as you can use it to evolve a few grass types that you might have, and you can sell the Carbos for extra cash.

Battles fought in Gearen Park are fought on the Grassy Terrain. As you may know, differrent Terrains have been introduced to the main series game, but have minimal effect on the battle. In Pokemon Rejuvenation, the field you battle in will heavily influence the battle, powering up moves, adjusting abilities, changing the type chart, and many other effects. If you are not familiar with Field Effects in general, I recommend you reading an overview about them here.

To the Sewers


With all the detours out of the way, we can finally continue the story. Heal up and either Chrisola Hotel or Gearen Lab, then head to the bottom right corner, past Gang Member Rufus, and talk to Ren and Venam. They'll talk about how Melia's underground figuring out the power issues, and invite you to join them in the sewers. Before you do that, head a little more down and you'll meet Karrina, the leader of the local gang. A little bit past her is a Reverse Candy.
